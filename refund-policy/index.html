The Implementation-Backed Refund Policy

For the Relapse Control Guide™. A process-backed refund policy for readers ready to implement faithfully under any clinical care they already have.

This is opt-in, not the default. You can purchase the Relapse Control Guide™ without this policy and still receive full access under standard terms. The policy is for the reader who wants both the guide and a protected commitment.

How the policy works

The policy has three tracks based on acne severity classification.

  1. Take the free Fit Assessment Quiz to confirm your track. Mild track readers self-assess inside the quiz using a standardised dermatology tool (the IGA), with photo review by Anna Cadano, RN, for refund-policy placement only. This is not a medical diagnosis. Moderate and Severe track readers confirm severity with their existing dermatologist.
  2. Sign the Refund Agreement within your track's window. Mild track: 3 days from purchase. Moderate or Severe: 7 days from purchase (the extra 4 days exist so you have time to secure dermatologist classification documentation).
  3. Submit baseline photographs within 24 hours of signing (front, left, right, with date verification).
  4. Receive your personalised Implementation Tracker and begin your window. Implement the guide's eleven tools at their prescribed cadences. Log honestly each week.
  5. At end of window, submit end-of-window photographs and your severity reassessment. If the compound refund trigger is met, you receive a full refund within 14 days.

The compound refund trigger

A refund is approved when all four of the following are true at the end of your Implementation Window:

  1. Baseline photographs were submitted in compliance.
  2. Your tracker shows consistent weekly logging plus documented usage of every required artefact at its prescribed cadence.
  3. End-of-window photographs were submitted in compliance.
  4. Both of these severity indicators are present:
    • The Six-Week Self-Review shows no observable improvement across its five indicators (less chaos, fewer rash decisions, better consistency, improved tolerability, possibly some early visible improvement);
    • And the end-of-window severity reassessment (Mild: nurse IGA reassessment; Moderate and Severe: dermatologist's written confirmation) shows no improvement in classified severity compared to baseline.

Why the trigger is compound

The Relapse Control Guide™ teaches that success in recurring acne is not only visual. Less chaos, fewer rash decisions, better consistency, improved tolerability — these are the early indicators that often precede visible clearing. Anchoring the refund only to "no visible improvement" would contradict the guide's own teaching. The compound trigger rewards real effort and stays true to what acne care actually looks like over time.

What we ask of you

  1. Severity classification per your tier.
  2. Complete product and substance disclosure at start of window — every item that touches the face area (skincare, makeup, hair products, lip products, toothpaste, body wash on jawline/chest, laundry detergent on pillowcases, phone hygiene).
  3. Completion of the Fit Assessment Quiz.
  4. Signed Refund Agreement within your track's window.
  5. Baseline photographs within 24 hours of signing.
  6. Honest weekly tracker logging throughout the window.
  7. Completion of the Six-Week Self-Review at the six-week mark.
  8. End-of-window photographs within 7 days of window end.
  9. End-of-window severity reassessment (nurse IGA for Mild, dermatologist confirmation for Moderate and Severe).
  10. Faithful implementation, defined by documented tracker usage of every required artefact at its prescribed cadence.

What we promise

  1. Immediate delivery of the Relapse Control Guide™ and your personalised Implementation Tracker on signing.
  2. IGA photo review (Mild track) within 48 hours of submission.
  3. Strict confidentiality on all submitted materials. Used solely for refund evaluation. Retained no longer than 12 months after refund claim resolution, then permanently deleted.
  4. Good-faith review of any refund claim within 14 calendar days of receiving complete documentation.

Disqualifying conditions

A refund claim is not approved if:

  • The tracker shows inconsistent implementation, prolonged gaps, missing artefact submissions, or evidence of substantial deviation.
  • The Six-Week Self-Review indicates improvement in two or more indicators.
  • The severity reassessment shows improvement.
  • The buyer's circumstances materially changed during the window and the buyer did not notify AnnAlchemy™.
  • Baseline or end-of-window photographs were not submitted in compliance.
  • The refund request was submitted outside the 14-day grace period after the window ends.
  • The buyer materially misrepresented their severity classification, treatment status, or product disclosure at intake.

Frequently asked questions

Why does the Mild track let me self-assess?

Mild acne is well-served by careful behavioural and routine education plus consistent over-the-counter care. Requiring a dermatologist visit just to qualify for a refund policy on a $27 educational guide would be an unnecessary financial barrier. The IGA gives Anna a defensible, standardised tool, and hard escalation triggers (photographic signs of moderate or severe acne, scarring, certain conditions) automatically route you to the Moderate-or-above track where dermatologist care is required.

What if Anna's photo review shows my acne is more than Mild?

You will receive an email confirming your track has been moved to Moderate or Severe, with the option to either obtain dermatologist classification documentation within 7 days and continue, or withdraw from the policy and retain the guide under standard terms.

Why do Moderate and Severe tracks require a dermatologist?

Moderate and Severe acne frequently benefit from prescription treatments that fall outside the educational scope of the Relapse Control Guide™. Without active dermatologist care, the policy would be asking the buyer to rely on the guide alone for presentations that often need more — neither fair to the buyer nor consistent with the guide's scope.

What counts as faithful implementation?

Faithful implementation is defined by documented tracker entries showing consistent weekly logging plus usage of every required artefact (eleven tools total in the guide) at the cadence the guide prescribes. The tracker is structured so that missing entries are visible at a glance — a buyer with consistent weekly entries and all required artefact submissions in place by end of window has met the threshold.

What if I see improvement in some indicators but not in classified severity?

The refund trigger is compound. It requires both the absence of improvement on the Six-Week Self-Review's five indicators AND the absence of improvement in classified severity. If either layer shows improvement, you are not eligible for refund — but the guide has done its job.

What if I become pregnant or change medication during my window?

Notify Anna in writing within 7 days of the change. Depending on the change, your window may be paused or the policy reset. Material changes that affect the implementation are accommodated in good faith; failing to notify is grounds for disqualification.

How is my information protected?

All materials submitted (photographs, tracker entries, dermatologist documentation, personal information) are confidential. Stored securely. Used solely for refund evaluation. Retained no longer than 12 months after refund claim resolution. Permanently deleted thereafter. You may request earlier deletion at any time.
